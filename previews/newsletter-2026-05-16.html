Today’s three money stories — and the third is the one no one is talking about yet.
One  ·  Markets

The 30-year Treasury just cracked 5.1%

The rate-cut story is unraveling fast. The 10-year climbed to 4.57%, the two-year sits at 4.07 — the whole curve moved. The highest 30-year print in nearly a year.

April’s $215B budget surplus sounded healthy until you saw that $97B of it went straight to interest on the debt — second only to Social Security.
Sources: reuters.com · treasury.gov
Two  ·  Business

Ben & Jerry’s parent is quietly dismantling what made the brand worth a billion

Magnum Ice Cream Co., the new parent after the Unilever spin-off, pulled $1.3B in revenue from Ben & Jerry’s alone — and is still down 25% from its February high. The company controls 21% of the global ice cream market.

In 2000, Ben & Jerry’s was worth $326M for one reason: the brand’s values posture. Magnum is unwinding that posture quarter by quarter. Investors haven’t priced what happens when the differentiator disappears.
Sources: ft.com · bloomberg.com
Three  ·  Science

MIT just lost a fifth of its incoming graduate class. The bill comes due in 2036.

Incoming graduate enrollment at MIT dropped 20% this year — about 500 fewer students. Federal research funding is down more than 20%. The endowment tax rate was hiked from 1.4% to 8% of gains, costing the school hundreds of millions annually.

Every lab that doesn’t fill this year is a patent, a drug, a chip design that doesn’t happen. The gap shows up in hiring a decade from now — quietly, in the productivity number nobody can explain.
Sources: mit.edu · insidehighered.com
